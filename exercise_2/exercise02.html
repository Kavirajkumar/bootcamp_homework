Travel blog to Italy

Italy, is the great place to visit. There is so much more to do and see in Italy.taly is well-known for its architecture, culture, art, opera, literature, film, and fashion. This is one of the best countries to go on a foreign tour. The country is known for its mesmerizing topography, pristine beaches, historical artifacts, monuments, and more.

Italy has over 3,000 museums. The national sport of Italy is soccer (known as football outside of America). Italy's national dish is pasta. The Italian language evolved from the Latin of the Roman Empire.


List of places to visit

  1. Rome

    2. Rome, the “Eternal City,” brims with ancient history, from the Colosseum to the port of Ostia Antica to majestic Vatican City and the Sistine Chapel. Because of its history , and beauty – and perhaps its gelato and pasta! – Rome is one of our most popular cities.

    Rome is the capital city of Italy. It is also the capital of the Lazio region, the centre of the Metropolitan City of Rome Capital, and a special comune (municipality) named Comune di Roma Capitale. With 2,860,009 residents in 1,285 km2 (496.1 sq mi),[2] Rome is the country's most populated comune and the third most populous city in the European Union by population within city limits. The Metropolitan City of Rome, with a population of 4,355,725 residents, is the most populous metropolitan city in Italy.



    Things to do in Rome:

    • Discovering Colosseum architecture
    • Pantheon Attractions
    • Trevi Fountain
    • Capitoline Museums
  2. Venice

    3. Venice is a treasure trove of art, from Renaissance paintings to iconic 20th-century pieces. There are a wealth of galleries and museums to visit, and you could easily spend each day of your holiday browsing a different one. The Scuola Grande di San Rocco is a great place to start your sightseeing.

    Both Venice are exceptional in their own right. Venice offers incredible architecture, romantic canals, and unique travel experiences.


    Things to do in Venice:

    • St. Mark's Basilica
    • Grand Canal
    • Gondola Rides
    • Rialto Bridge
  3. Florence

    4. Florence was a centre of medieval European trade and finance and one of the wealthiest cities of that era. It is considered by many academics to have been the birthplace of the Renaissance, becoming a major artistic, cultural, commercial, political, economic and financial center.

    It is a destination deserving of a spot on every travel enthusiast's bucket list. The charm of Florence lies not just in its famous landmarks and artistic heritage but also in the myriad of personal experiences it offers.


    Things to do in Florence:

    • Piazza della Signoria
    • Duomo
    • Ponte Vecchio
    • Galleria dell'Accademia
  4. Tuscany

    5. Tuscany is famous for its rolling hills, picturesque vineyards, and medieval towns. You can rent a car or join a tour to explore the countryside and visit charming towns like Siena, San Gimignano, and Montepulciano.

    It's no surprise then that walking in Tuscany is one of the most popular activities for visitors to the area, who come to walk the historical routes and newly established pathways that link together towns, cities and rural settlements that are nestled into the Tuscan landscape.


    Things to do in Tuscany:

    • Piazzale Michelangalo
    • Piaza Del Duomo
    • Leaning tower of Pisa
    • Galleria degli Uffizi
